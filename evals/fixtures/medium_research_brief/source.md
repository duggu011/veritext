# Helios Renewables — Q1 2026 Snapshot

Prepared by Lila Okafor, VP of Research, on April 14, 2026.
Distribution: Helios board of directors. Confidential.

## 1. Headlines

Q1 2026 revenue $482.3 million, up 17.4% versus $410.8 million in Q1 2025. Adjusted EBITDA $94.1 million at 19.5% margin, beating the $88.0 million forecast. Free cash flow positive at $12.6 million for the first time in eight quarters.

## 2. Operations

Fleet generation 7,318 gigawatt-hours, up 11.2% from 6,581 in Q1 2025. Solar capacity factor 28.4% versus 29.0% target. Atacama-1 in Chile commenced operation January 22, 2026, contributing 312 gigawatt-hours.

## 3. Acquisition

On March 28, 2026, the board approved acquiring Northwind Storage, a Reno, Nevada battery operator, for $1.24 billion. Closing expected by September 30, 2026, subject to FERC and CFIUS review. Northwind operates 1.85 gigawatt-hours across seven U.S. states.

## 4. Guidance

On the April 9, 2026 earnings call, CEO Marcus Bell committed to at least 4.2 gigawatts of new solar capacity by December 31, 2027, with at least 60% under long-term offtake at signing. Full-year 2026 revenue guidance reaffirmed at $2.10 to $2.25 billion.

## 5. Risk

The U.S. Department of Commerce raised module countervailing duties from 15.0% to 26.5% on March 6, 2026. Approximately 18% of the 2026 module pipeline is exposed.

## 6. Personnel

Dr. Anya Kowalski appointed Chief Sustainability Officer on February 2, 2026. Director Hiroshi Tanaka announced retirement effective at the June 18, 2026 Annual Meeting.

## 7. Distractors — Do Not Extract

CleanTech Daily's "Helios planning a 10 GW deployment by 2027" headline was unauthorized and incorrect. A Northwind/Sunbelt draft term sheet from January 2026 was never executed.
